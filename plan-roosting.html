What kind of site is this?
First identify whether the roost is in a natural habitat or a man-made structure, because the best management strategy depends on site type.
Natural site
Natural roosts are usually ecologically important, so the default strategy is protection rather than removal.
Man-made site
Man-made sites can create direct conflict with people through guano, smell, noise, and public concern.
Conserve and protect
If the site is natural, prioritize conservation, reduce disturbance, and preserve their natural habitat when possible.
Use in tourism to conserve bat populations while providing social and economic benefits
Protected bat sites can support ecotourism and education, which can help fund conservation and improve public attitudes.
Is the site in need to repelling the bats?
If a man-made site creates major nuisance or safety issues, then active intervention may be needed. If conflict is low, monitoring and warning people may be enough.
Yes: Use a form of bat repellant while guiding them to a near by alternative site
If intervention is necessary, combine exclusion using repellants with an alternative roost so the bats are redirected rather than simply displaced.
No: Warn civilians about bat presence in the area, check on the sites once in a time period
If the site does not require removal, inform the public using signs, reduce panic, and use periodic monitoring to prevent future problems.
