Ways to decrease Public Stigma
Public stigma often comes from fear and misinformation. The goal is to replace myths with simple, accurate, easy-to-remember guidance so people respond safely and don’t harm bats.
Post videos on platforms such as TikTok for educational purposes
Short videos reach lots of people quickly. They’re good for “Myth vs Fact” content, safe-response steps (“don’t touch bats”), and explaining why bats are helpful (insect control).
Sell merchandise that has interesting graphics and educational phrases
Merch can spread positive messages and normalize bats. If you do it, keep the messaging simple and factual, and use profits to support education or mitigation projects.
Handout pamphlets about how to correctly view bats
Pamphlets/signage work well at places where conflict happens (bridges, parks, schools). Include: (1) do not handle bats, (2) who to call if a bat is inside, (3) fun bat facts to reduce fear, and (4) why bats matter to the ecosystem.
